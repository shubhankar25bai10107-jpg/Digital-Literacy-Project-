## 🧑‍🎓 About the Project

This repository is my official submission for the **CSE0001 – Digital Literacy** course at **VIT Bhopal University**.  
The project simulates my role as a **Student Digital Ambassador**, where I guide my peers in building a safe, professional, and effective digital presence.

The portfolio is built around five core tasks, one for each module of the course:

1. Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic  
2. Student Digital Portfolio setup  
3. Coding and collaboration platforms exploration  
4. Professional email and social media etiquette  
5. Cybercrime awareness case study and prevention guide  

All work is organized in a single GitHub repository, which itself becomes a live example of a professional online portfolio.

## 🎨 Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic

In **Task 1**, I created a one-page **Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic** using an online design tool (such as Canva or a similar platform).  
The infographic introduces the concept of digital literacy, highlights essential tools for students, explains safe internet practices, and emphasizes building a professional online presence and good email etiquette.

The design is organised into clear sections so that a first-year student can quickly understand:

- What digital literacy means in simple, student-friendly terms  
- Practical digital tools that can boost productivity and learning  
- Basic rules for safe browsing, password management, and privacy  
- Key do’s and don’ts of professional online communication  

The final infographic is exported as an image and stored in the `task-1-presentation/` folder, so faculty and batchmates can easily view it directly from this repository.

In **Task 3**, the focus shifts to **hands-on coding practice** and **cloud-based collaboration**, which are essential skills for engineering students.  
I explored at least one coding platform and used Google’s tools to design and manage a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz.

From the coding side:

## ✉️ Task 4 – Email Etiquette & Social Media

**Task 4** assesses my ability to apply **professional email etiquette** and promote responsible social media use.  
I wrote two complete professional emails and prepared a social media do’s and don’ts checklist for college students.

The two emails include:

1. An email from a student to a professor requesting an **extension** on an assignment deadline.  
2. An email from a student to an **internship coordinator**, expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity.

Both emails follow important email etiquette rules:

- Clear and specific subject lines  
- Professional greetings and closings  
- Concise, polite, and well-organised body content  
- Proper sign-off with my name and relevant details  

These drafts are saved as text or document files inside the `task-4-email-etiquette/` folder.

In addition, I created a **Social Media Do’s and Don’ts Checklist** with at least:

- 5 Do’s (positive behaviours to follow)  
- 5 Don’ts (risky behaviours to avoid)

The checklist is stored as `social-media-checklist.md` in the same folder and can be shared with classmates as a quick reference for responsible online behaviour.

## 🛡 Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention

**Task 5** is focused on **Cybercrime Awareness**, where I studied a specific cybercrime type relevant to students and created a case study along with a prevention guide.  
The selected crime type can be phishing, UPI/online payment fraud, identity theft, cyberbullying, or ransomware.

For this task:

- I wrote a **200–250 word case study** describing what the cybercrime is, how it usually happens (step-by-step), who is typically targeted, and what the consequences can be.  
- This case study is saved as `casestudy.md` or `casestudy.pdf` inside the `task-5-cybercrime/` folder.

I also created a **“Stay Safe Online” prevention checklist** with at least eight specific and actionable tips designed for college students in India, including:

- Safe behaviour for social media and messaging  
- Extra care with links, attachments, and unknown contacts  
- At least two tips focused on **UPI/online payment safety**  

The checklist clearly mentions the official reporting channels:

- **National Cyber Crime Portal**: https://cybercrime.gov.in  
- **Cyber Crime Helpline**: 1930  

This checklist is stored as `prevention-checklist.md` and can be used as a ready-made awareness resource.
